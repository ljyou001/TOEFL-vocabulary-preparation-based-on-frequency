Why does the woman go to see the man?

  • A. To ask for his help in getting a job off campus

  • B. To apply for a job in the library archives

  • C. To find out if the man has received her letter of reference

  • D. To get help on a project she is doing with a professor

显示答案 正确答案: A

    NARRATOR:Listen to part of a conversation between a student and a university librarian.

    FEMALE STUDENT:Mr. Reese? I'm Jennifer Lee. I-I worked on the photography exhibition last year, the one that was commemorating the university's 100th anniversary.I helped select and display photos for the exhibit; I helped set it all up, remember?

    MALE LIBRARIAN:Oh, sure... you're the photography student that Professor Ryan sent to us. How have you been?

    FEMALE STUDENT:Good, thanks. And I, ah...well, I was hoping I could talk to you about a job, actually.

    MALE LIBRARIAN:[surprised] Oh, the job here in the library archive? That was fast; we just posted it yesterday.

    FEMALE STUDENT:Oh, no, actually it's for a photography job...in the portrait studio at the mall.

    MALE LIBRARIAN:Taking portrait photos? Sounds like a great job for a photography student. But, how can I help?

    FEMALE STUDENT:Well, I went for an interview. But I still need to give them professional references from people I've worked for.And...well, I don't have a lot of work experience, but, you know, you've-you've seen how- how I helped plan and carry a project through, and showed up on time and everything.So, I was kinda hoping I- y'know, I could give them your name as someone they could talk to?

    MALE LIBRARIAN:Of course. We all thought you did a great job.Ah, I remember saying that to Professor Ryan, and he thought so, too.I suppose you've already asked him for a recommendation?

    FEMALE STUDENT:I have. He's actually the one who convinced me to apply for it.I wasn't sure I should. I mean, having real photography experience would be great for my resume.But...well, I'm a little concerned about the hours.It's ten hours a week, which is fine, but they're all during the daytime.I'd have to schedule several classes for the late afternoon and evening.I'm not sure I'll be able to do that.

    MALE LIBRARIAN:Yeah, I see how that could be a problem. [thought occurs] Well, maybe you should consider the job I mentioned.We just got a grant to hire a part-time library assistant for our photography archives.It doesn't involve taking pictures, but it does involve working with pictures...um, filling requests for specific photos from students and professors.And it's in the evenings, from 6 to 9, three days a week.

    FEMALE STUDENT:Hmm...well, the hours are definitely better. But I really do wanna find out about that job at the studio first...I'm sure you understand.

    MALE LIBRARIAN:Of course. But if it doesn't work out, keep us in mind.

  • 旁白：请听一个学生和一个大学图书馆管理员之间的对话。

    学生：Reese先生，我是Jennifer Lee，我去年在摄影展上工作过，就是那个纪念学校成立100周年的摄影展。我帮忙挑选和陈列了展览用的照片，我帮忙设置的整个影展，还记得吗？

    图书管理员：哦，当然记得啦！你是Ryan教授派给我们的那个摄影专业的学生。你还好吗？

    学生：很好，谢谢。我……事实上我希望能跟你聊一聊一份工作的事情。

    图书管理员：哦，我们图书馆档案室的那份工作？哈，你也太快了！我们昨天才发布的消息呢。

    学生：啊，不是。实际上是商场里艺术摄影馆的一个摄影师工作。

    图书管理员：拍摄艺术照？听起来对摄影专业的学生来说是一个很棒的工作。可是我能怎么帮到你呢？

    学生：我去面试过了，但我还需要给他们提供我之前效劳过的人写的专业推荐信。但是我没有多少工作经验。可是，你看到过我是怎么帮忙筹备，怎么完成一个项目，并且准时出勤等等之类的。所以，我有点希望……你知道的，能把你的名字告诉他们，作为他们能谈话的对象。

    图书管理员：当然可以啦，我们都觉得你工作得非常棒。我还记得跟Ryan教授说过，而且他也是这么认为的。我猜你已经找他推荐过了？

    学生：是的。实际上是他说服我申请这份工作的。我当时不太确定应不应该申请，我是说，能有真正的摄影工作经验会给我的简历增色不少。但是……我有点担心工作时间。一星期要工作十个小时，这本来也没什么，但这些时间都是在白天。我必须把好几门课排到下午晚点时候和晚上。我不确定我能做到。

    图书管理员：是的，我知道这会有多麻烦。也许你应该考虑一下我提到的那份工作。我们刚刚得到一笔拨款，为我们的摄影档案雇一个兼职的图书馆助理。虽然不涉及拍照片，但确实能和照片一起工作，满足学生和教授的具体照片要求。而且工作时间都在晚上，从6点到9点，一星期三天。

    学生：工作时间当然更好一些，但是我真的想先试试看摄影馆的那份工作。我相信你会理解的。

    图书管理员：当然啦。但是如果没成功，记得来找我们。
